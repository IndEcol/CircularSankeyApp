A large amount of statistical data on flows and stocks of energy, material, and capital in different industrial processes is available. These statistical information is abstract and they are very comprehensive and complex. Therefore, they are presented in aggregated form to decision makers. This aggregation allows them to answer central questions on a country's metabolism like: How much of the total energy consumed goes into mining/making materials/transport? or How material and energy intensive is the service sector of this country?" To present these data in a aggregated way, a good and flexible option could be transforming the data into graphical or pictorial form which we refer by the term 'Data Visualization'.

Data Visualization is a way of representing abstract data (machine readable) into human readable form. The target of this visualization is to enable the decision makers to see data visually, so that they can grasp difficult concepts easily. Data visualization is a fast and easy way to present concepts and you can identify areas that need attention or improvement.

A new concept of data visualization is based on the widely used Sankey diagram. A Sankey diagram is a type of flow diagram where the flow is represented by arrows of varying thickness depending on the quantity of flow. They are often used to visualize energy, material or any other industrial or business flow and are especially useful in demonstrating proportionality to a flow where different parts of the diagram represent different quantities in a system.


The core of the master project is to develop an open source piece of software that converts a well-defined dataset (specified as .csv) into a scalable vector graphic (.svg) using one of several pre-defined possible structures of the diagram (specified as .xml). The idea is to start with the Sankey method of D3.js and extend it to a rich tool that can be used by researchers in the field of environmental systems analysis in the years to come.
